Historical importance

The city and the administartive center of Jambyl Region in Kazakhstan, located on the Talas (Taraz) River in the south of the country near the border with Kyrgyzstan.

Geographic location

  1. Tajikistan
  2. Kyrgyzstan
  3. Almaty
  4. Shymkent

Population

It had a population of 330 1000 as of the 1999 census, up 9% from 1989, making it one of the fastest-growing cities in the country, after Astana and Turkistan.

Nature

The city is full of small lakes and rivers which provide local fauna with nutrients and vegitation.

The region has more than 3,000 plant species. The total area of the hunting ground is 13.9 thousand hectares, where more than 40 species of animals live. The fish farm, covering an area of 27.8 thousand ha, consists of 74 reservoirs, of which 73 are designated for fishing.
